### Hi, welcome to my GitHub! <img src="https://emojis.slackmojis.com/emojis/images/1643515585/16060/unicorn.gif" width="25"/>

I'm **Amy**, a software engineer, dancer, and artist.

I'm currently a Software Engineer at [**@Google**](https://github.com/google) working at the forefront of **AI Search**, where I develop next-generation features across Travel and Food for millions of daily global users. My work adds new features directly to the Google Search page and improves LLM accuracy for millions of complex user searches, such as "hotels in Las Vegas for kids who love shows" or "Japanese restaurants in Mountain View."

Previously, I was at [**@Amazon**](https://github.com/aws) (Ads) and [**@BlackBerry**](https://github.com/blackberry) (AI Cybersecurity).

I’ve also contributed to the [**Android Open Source Project**](https://android-review.googlesource.com/q/amyouyang).

Committed to making tech more accessible, I lead the Women in Google Search community, mentor Mandarin-speaking women in STEM, and develop CS curricula for students in underserved communities.

I graduated from The University of Texas at Austin with a Computer Science Honors degree and I have a lifelong passion for teaching, spanning everything from data structures to dance and calligraphy.


Outside of work, I’m fascinated by the intersection of creativity, art, and code. I design and code games from scratch – hand-drawing and hand-animating every asset myself, including:
- a gesture-tracking computer vision program that mathematically generates 3D geometric and particle visualizations simulating anime magic spells based on the user’s hand movements (inspired by Olympian Alysa Liu),
- a "gacha" wellness game where users build magical potions and unlock fantasy characters by drinking water,
- a K-pop Demon Hunters x Pokémon dance battle game
- and much more.

By using "girly," cute aesthetics and pop culture themes, I aim to show girls that engineering is a creative tool that relates to their interests and that coding is cool, cute, and fun. :)
